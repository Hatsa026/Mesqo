The ingredient

The seedpod the
world forgot.

Long before coffee reached the West, indigenous communities across the Sonoran and Chihuahuan deserts were grinding mesquite pods into flour, bread, and drinks. Naturally sweet, naturally caffeine-free, and a dietary staple for thousands of years.

Today we roast those same pods the way coffee is roasted - slow, low, patient. The result is something that looks and brews like espresso but comes from an entirely different plant, with an entirely different nutritional story.

Why Mesqo

What's actually
in it.

Mesqo is a different category entirely - a roasted mesquite espresso that happens to be naturally caffeine-free. Here's what's in the pod.

Naturally caffeine-free
Not decaffeinated - there's simply no caffeine in the mesquite pod to begin with. No solvents, no extraction, no trace amounts.
High in dietary fibre
Mesquite pods are roughly 25% fibre by composition. That's high for any roasted ingredient - and something coffee and decaf can't offer at all.
Mineral content
Contains calcium, magnesium, potassium, zinc and iron - naturally present in the pod, not added in.
Low acidity
Gentler on the stomach than coffee. If acidity is the reason you've cut coffee out, mesquite espresso gives you the ritual back.
A long nutritional history
Mesquite pods have been eaten as a staple food for thousands of years. Traditionally valued for steady, sustained energy - and increasingly studied for blood-sugar effects.
